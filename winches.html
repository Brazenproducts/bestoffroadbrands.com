Best Winch Brands

A winch can be the difference between driving home and calling a tow truck. This is not the component to cheap out on. Here are the winch brands that have earned trust through decades of recoveries.

1

Warn

Warn has been building winches since 1948. That's 75+ years of engineering, testing, and refining the product that defines their brand. The ZEON series is the current flagship — powerful, efficient, and beautifully engineered. The VR EVO series offers excellent performance at a more accessible price. Warn's electric winches deliver consistent pulling power with outstanding thermal management, and their synthetic rope options have improved safety across the industry. There is no better winch brand. Period.

Known for: Industry standard since 1948, ZEON and VR EVO series, unmatched reliability

2

Smittybilt

The Smittybilt X2O and XRC winch lines offer solid pulling power at roughly half the price of comparable Warn units. For the budget-conscious builder, this is the sweet spot of performance vs. cost. Will they last as long as a Warn under heavy use? Probably not. But for occasional recovery use, they're more than adequate and the value proposition is strong.

Known for: Budget-friendly winches, good performance per dollar, X2O series
Visit Smittybilt →Visit Store →
3

Factor 55

Factor 55 doesn't make complete winches, but they make the best winch accessories on the planet. Their FlatLink and ProLink replaceable fairlead links are CNC-machined from 6061 aluminum and replace the clunky hooks that come standard on most winches. The UltraHook is another best-in-class product. If you have any winch, Factor 55 accessories make it better and safer.

Known for: CNC-machined winch accessories, FlatLink, ProLink, UltraHook
